Zone Attribute
  1. Observed Level Cycling (OLC) refers to the amount of current cycling activity according to the Census data
  2. Scenario-based Level of Cycling (SLC) is the expected rate of cycling in given area (comes from the underlying scenario)
  3. Scenario-based Increase in Cycling (SIC) is the number of additional trips or cyclists that can be expected (the difference between the Observed and the Scenario cases)

Direct Route

The red line represents a direct route from the centre of two zones. Once the user clicks on it, following information is displayed:
  1. Total commutes - the total number of flows between these two zones
  2. Observed (OLC) - according to the census how many observed flows are between these two zones
  3. Scenario (SLC) - according to the underlying scenario, how many flows are between these two zones
  4. Increase (SLC - OLC) - the difference between observed and scenario flows
  5. Route Distance - the route distance in km between the centre of the zones
  6. Type of Route - red colour represents direct route
Quiet Route

Similar to direct route, we also calculate the quiet route between zones. The blue line represents it. Once the user clicks on it, following information is displayed:
  1. Total commutes - the total number of flows between these two zones
  2. Observed (OLC) - according to the census how many observed flows are between these two zones
  3. Scenario (SLC) - according to the underlying scenario, how many flows are between these two zones
  4. Increase (SLC - OLC) - the difference between observed and scenario flows
  5. Route Distance - the route distance in km between the centre of the zones
  6. Type of Route - blue colour represents quite route
Straight Lines

Similar to routes, we also display the direct distance between the centre of zones. It is displayed by a maroon  colour. Once a user clicks on it, a popup is displayed with following information
  1. Total commutes - the total number of flows between these two zones
  2. Observed (OLC) - according to the census how many observed flows are between these two zones
  3. Scenario (SLC) - according to the underlying scenario, how many flows are between these two zones
  4. Increase (SLC - OLC) - the difference between observed and scenario flows
  5. Distance - the direct distance in km between the centre of the zones
Zone Information

Zone informatio is also calculated and can be seen once user clicks on the centre of the them - represented by a black centroid. Following information is displayed:
  1. Zone: Zone name according to the Census
  2. OLC: Observed level of cycling according to the Census
  3. Hilliness: Shows the hilliness attribute of the zone
Legend

We also display a legend which shows the quartile of level of cycling, depending on the scenario user has selected. It shows the title of the scenario, and also:
  1. The number of flows for each quartile
  2. The colour assinged to each quartile
