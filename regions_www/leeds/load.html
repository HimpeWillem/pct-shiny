Input flow data

There are 15375 flows with origins and destinations in the study area. Of these, 2312 meet the criteria that 30 people reported travelling between these two areas in the 2011 Census (a modifiable parameter used to reduce the computational requirements of the model). That is 15.04% of flows, accounting for 68.75% of commutes in the study area.

Distance distributions

The distance distribution of trips in the study area is displayed in the figure below, which compares the result with the distribution of trips nationwide.

Distance distribution of all trips in study lines (blue) compared with national average (dotted bars)

From the nationwide sample of trips, 51.5% of trips are less than 5km.

In the case study area 59.38% of sampled trips are less than 5km.

Subsetting by distance (set to 15 km) and removing inter-zone flows further reduces the number of flows from 2312 to 2169.

The flow model

To estimate the potential rate of cycling under different scenarios regression models operating at the flow level are used. These can be seen in the model script which is available online.

Cycling in the study area

The overall rate of cycling in the flows in the study area (after subsetting for distance) is 4.6%, compared a rate from the national data (of equally short flows) of 5.0%.
