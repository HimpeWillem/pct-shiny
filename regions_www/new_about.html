About

This is the online home of the open source transport planning system, the Propensity to Cycle Tool (PCT). The PCT is released under the AGP Licence: it is free to use, copy and modify, e.g.Â to create versions for new cities and states.

The PCT was created by an academic team from CEDAR (University of Cambridge), the Institute for Transport Studies (ITS)/CDRC (University of Leeds), the University of Westminster, and YouthinMind. The work was initially funded by the Department for Transport (DfT) to create the National Propensity to Cycle Tool for England (PCT-England). This will be officially launched in July 2016.

Further information on the project can be found at the University of Cambridge, the University of Leeds and the European Cycling Federation.
